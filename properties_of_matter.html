Understanding the properties of materials is essential in mechanical engineering, especially when designing engine components.

Physical Properties

These include characteristics like density and thermal conductivity, which affect an engine's weight and heat management.

Chemical Properties

These determine how materials react with other substances, such as their resistance to corrosion, which is crucial for engine durability.

Material behavior

Brittle Materials: Strong but prone to sudden failure under stress. Used where compressive strength is important.

Ductile Materials: Can bend or stretch without breaking, making them ideal for parts that need to absorb energy or endure deformation.
